## Design Context

### Users
Frequent travelers — truckers, transportation professionals, construction workers, and people on the go who need to quickly assess environmental risks at their current or destination location. They're checking the app in transit, often on mobile, possibly in bright outdoor conditions. The job to be done: **know if it's safe to go there and what to prepare for**, fast.

### Brand Personality
**Prepared. Slick. Confident.**

RiskRadar should feel like a trusted, premium tool — not a utility or a weather widget. It communicates authority without alarm. The tone is calm and assured: "we've got this covered." Users should feel safe, informed, and in control.

**Emotional goals:** Calm, trust, safety, confidence.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Minimal, slick, and clean. Reduce visual noise. Let data breathe.
- **NOT a weather app.** Avoid radar maps, forecast grids, animated clouds, or any trope that screams "weather." RiskRadar is a risk intelligence tool, not a forecast viewer.
- **Color palette:** Shift from blue to **green and creamy/warm neutral** tones. Earthy, grounded, and calming — reinforcing safety and preparedness.
- **No dark mode.** Light mode only. Focus on getting one mode polished and premium.
- **Typography:** Clean, confident. Favor clarity and hierarchy over decoration.
- **Spacing:** Generous whitespace. Cards and sections should feel spacious, not cramped.

### Design Principles

1. **Calm over alarm.** Even when showing critical risks, the UI should feel composed and trustworthy — not panicked. Severity is communicated through clear hierarchy and color coding, not visual chaos.

2. **Minimal and intentional.** Every element earns its place. No decorative clutter, no redundant information. If it doesn't help the user make a decision, remove it.

3. **Slick and premium.** Smooth transitions, consistent spacing, refined typography. The app should feel polished — like a tool made by people who care about craft.

4. **Glanceable intelligence.** Users are in transit. Information must be scannable in seconds. Lead with what matters, let users drill down if they want more.

5. **Accessible by default.** WCAG AA compliance. High contrast on light backgrounds, clear touch targets, readable type sizes. Designed for real-world use including bright outdoor conditions.
